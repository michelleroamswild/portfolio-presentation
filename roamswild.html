About Me

I roamed long before I built it.

I spend a lot of my time outside — hiking trails, sleeping in tents, and chasing long road trips through the kinds of places that don't show up well on most maps. RoamsWild grew straight out of the gap between the tools I was using and the experience I actually wanted on the road.

Project Overview

Objective

Explore how AI tools and modern APIs extend capabilities as a Product Designer. AI functioned as a practical build partner for rapid exploration of system architecture, API design, and data transformations.

Target Users

Road-based travelers, dispersed camping enthusiasts, landscape photographers, and public lands explorers who need smarter location intelligence, not just listings.

From Ideas to Systems

Decision-support, not just listings

I started with simple heuristics — combining public land data with road networks to identify dead ends and intersections where dispersed campsites often appear. From there, I designed and built scoring systems that surface what actually matters to travelers and photographers.

Terrain Lighting Quality

Predicts optimal lighting conditions using sun azimuth calculations, weather patterns, and terrain orientation data.

Scenic Potential Scoring

Evaluates landscape diversity, elevation changes, water features, and geological interest to surface hidden gems.

Access Confidence

Assesses road conditions, land ownership boundaries, seasonal restrictions, and vehicle requirements.

Timing Relevance

Surfaces the best windows for visiting based on weather, seasonal conditions, and golden hour calculations.

Takeaway 03

With the range of APIs available today, deriving new data can unlock entirely new product experiences.

Outcomes

What I Learned

AI as a practical build partner — not a novelty. Claude Code accelerated prototyping for API endpoints, scoring logic, and data transformations that would have taken significantly longer solo.

Design still drives the experience. Without intentional design systems, AI-generated UIs converge on the same generic patterns. A strong foundation made the difference.

Derived data creates new product categories. Combining weather, terrain, and timing APIs produced intelligence that doesn't exist in any single data source.

Real domain expertise matters. Personal experience with outdoor travel, camping, and photography informed every scoring decision — AI amplified judgment, it didn't replace it.
