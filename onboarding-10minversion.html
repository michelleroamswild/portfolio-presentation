The Audit

What data is needed when, what we can automate, and where the efficiencies are.

Before redesigning anything, my PM and I audited all 40+ inputs the old flow collected. We mapped every field on three axes:

01

What it was used for internally

02

Whether it was required to create an account

03

Which role was expected to provide it — engineer, business owner, or finance

Scope & Sequencing

Choosing velocity over perfection to unlock immediate value.

01

Scoping for Certainty

When scoping the MVP, we looked at our open-opportunity GMV across platforms and weighed it against where an automated interface would actually accelerate the onboarding timeline.

02

Experience First

We prioritized replacing the legacy form and manual contact upfront to transform the end-user experience immediately. We deliberately chose not to wait on engineering for full automated provisioning.

The Ripple

Onboarding couldn't be optimized in isolation.

Cross-team collaboration & prioritization
Payments Merchant Dashboard Checkout Everywhere
01 / 03
Merchant Dashboard

Deferring data collection changed the merchant's first-time experience in the dashboard. New in-product guidance had to be designed in lockstep with the new flow — and it scaled to cover setup tasks for other products too.

02 / 03
Payments Setup

Pulling payment data out of onboarding meant it had to go somewhere else. So it moved into the Payments team's own setup flow, where merchants already had the context to answer it.

03 / 03
Scalable by Design

Not in the original spec: the flow was built to flex across a growing set of product integrations and installation paths — Checkout, Ignite, Checkout Everywhere, and whatever came next.
