Case Study

From 70+ days
to Minutes

Designing a scalable, automated merchant onboarding system for Bolt Products

Michelle Taylor — Lead Product Designer

Project Overview

My Role

Lead Product Designer working with Product Manager and Engineering Lead, a pod of engineers on implementation, Sales and Implementation Managers

Goal

Increase merchant self-service experiences, reduce internal touchpoints and manual work to get merchants live faster. Cut down time-to-live to act on potential revenue in open opportunities.

Challenges

• Change resistance from internal teams hesitant to adopt new processes. 

• Data validation complexity across multiple teams and products. 
• Platform variability across e-commerce integrations (Magento, Adobe Commerce).

Key Metric

• Increased onboarding efficiency as perceived by IMs

• Reduced time to launch
• Increase SI Deals
• Increased deal velocity

Why This Mattered

Onboarding Was Blocking Revenue

Onboarding wasn't just a UX problem – it was a systems bottleneck impacting revenue velocity, internal team capacity, and the very first impression merchants had of Bolt. Every day of delay meant lost momentum on deals and strained resources.

70+ days
Average time from contract signing to merchant go-live
50+ days
Even after account creation, merchants still waited weeks to go live
5+ email cycles
Back-and-forth between merchant and Bolt teams before setup complete
Heavy IM dependency
Implementation Managers manually handling tasks that could be automated
$1.4B at stake
Adobe Commerce opportunities gated by onboarding friction
Key Results
70+
Days cut down to just a few minutes for merchant account creation time
40 → 10
Inputs condensed, removing redundant data collection, and targeting correct users
$1.4B
In open opportunities by removing months off go-live time for signing merchants (for Adobe Commerce alone)

Fewer direct touchpoints and intervention with merchants during onboarding. The sales team gained greater flexibility with how they engage prospects.

Strategic Approach

Phased Epics

Epic 1

Redesign Onboarding Form

Create a scalable, simplified, and consistent flow for collecting the essential information needed to create a Bolt merchant account.

Epic 2

Automate Account Creation

Automate account creation and configuration across all shopping cart platforms, removing manual steps previously handled by Bolt teams.

Epic 3

Auto-Link Adobe Commerce

Enable automatic linking between Bolt accounts and Adobe Commerce — the highest-impact shopping platform for revenue opportunity.

Adjustments Along the Way
Moving payment info from onboarding into the merchant dashboard
Designing task completion UI for post-account creation steps
Adding international address support for expanding merchant base
Adding screens and flows for new standalone Bolt products
The Problem
Basic onboarding was taking over 2 months after contract signing.

Time-consuming communication loops between Merchants and Bolt

Manual task handling by Bolt's Implementation managers

Overly long forms requiring merchants to gather info from different stakeholders

Repetitive data entry by 3+ different merchant users

Address fields didn't support international addresses

5+ contact points between Bolt and Merchant done through email

Poor accessibility design resulting in hard to read information

Redundant payment and configuration data causing blockers

Discovery

Auditing 40+ Inputs

My PM and I collaborated with cross-functional teams to audit every input collected during onboarding. We identified which fields were truly required, who the intended user was, and how data was used internally.

  • What data was used for internally
  • Whether it was necessary for account creation
  • Which user role was expected to provide it

Redesigned Flow

Data translated into the desired end-to-end flow, clarifying surfaces, required inputs, and sequence handling.

Long-Term Vision

Outcomes

Impact

The redesigned onboarding launched successfully — merchants can now create accounts and begin setup in minutes instead of months
