Case Study

From 70+ Days
to Minutes

A redesign of Bolt's merchant onboarding system — turning a months-long manual bottleneck into a self-serve flow that takes minutes.

The Problem

Onboarding was the business's biggest bottleneck.

Merchants would sign — then wait weeks, sometimes months, before they could actually start processing payments. Internally, every step required manual work across ops, risk, and compliance.

70+ day timelines

Going live took longer than most product launches.

Heavy manual review

Every merchant ran through ops, risk, and compliance by hand.

Merchants blocked

Signed deals stalled before generating a single transaction.

Revenue delayed

Open opportunities sat untouched while merchants waited.

Why it broke

It wasn't a screen problem. It was a system problem.

  • Fragmented systems and tools across teams
  • Sequential verification — every step waited on the last
  • Heavy dependency on internal handoffs

The Strategy

Reframe the system, not the steps.

Shift 01
Manual → Automated

Replace human review with automated checks wherever risk and compliance allow.

Shift 02
Sequential → Parallel

Run verification, underwriting, and provisioning concurrently instead of waiting in a chain.

Shift 03
Internal → Self-Serve

Hand control to the merchant. Internal teams move from gatekeepers to safety net.

Tradeoffs

Speed had a price tag. We knew it.

This wasn't just a design exercise — it required real tradeoffs between business goals, operational risk, and merchant experience. We made these decisions deliberately.

Automation vs fraud risk

How much could we automate before increasing exposure? Some checks stayed manual on purpose.

Speed vs verification accuracy

Faster verification meant accepting tighter signal. We backed it with stronger downstream monitoring.

Flexibility vs standardization

We intentionally limited custom paths to make the flow scalable across products and merchants.

The Solution

A system, not a stack of screens.

Account creation became instant. Verification ran automatically. Checks that used to wait in line now ran in parallel — and manual intervention became the exception, not the default.

For merchants

  • Far fewer back-and-forth communications
  • A single, clear path to live
For the business

Faster revenue capture

  • Closed deals start generating revenue sooner
  • Onboarding scales without scaling headcount
  • Sales can demo a real working flow
For internal teams

Reduced manual workload

  • Implementation managers freed from rote tasks
  • Risk + compliance only review exceptions
  • Higher-value work moves to the front
What I Learned

The highest-impact design work isn't about UI.

It's about rethinking systems, aligning teams, and designing inside heavy constraints — not because constraints are fun, but because they're where the real leverage lives.

Design within constraints

Compliance and risk aren't blockers — they're the boundary conditions that make the design defensible and shippable.

Systems, not screens

The biggest wins came from redesigning how the pieces connected, not from polishing individual flows.

Aligning teams = the work

Most of the hard work was getting risk, ops, and engineering to agree on a new model — design followed.

What's Next

If I picked this back up tomorrow.

Idea 01

Expand automation coverage

Push automation deeper into the edge cases that still drop into manual review queues.

Idea 02

Smarter risk-based routing

Send merchants down different paths based on real-time risk signals instead of one shared flow.

Idea 03

Better edge case handling

Treat the exceptions as a designed experience instead of a fallback — clear status, clear next steps.
